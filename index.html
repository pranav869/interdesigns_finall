How It Works

Process of Work

01

Initial Meeting

Understand customer requirements and expectations. Discuss project scope and budget.

02

Customer Enquiry

Receive enquiry through call, message, referral, or visit. Gather basic project details.

03

Appointment Scheduling

Fix a convenient date and time for site visit or consultation.

04

Space Layout Discussion

Measure and analyze the available space. Discuss functional requirements and space planning.

05

Design Concept Discussion

Share general design ideas, themes, materials, and finishes. Understand the customer's style preferences.

06

Estimated Quote Presentation

Present a preliminary cost estimate based on requirements. Explain the scope of work included.

07

Proposal Finalization

Freeze the design concept, scope, and commercial terms. Confirm timelines and deliverables.

08

Advance Payment

Collect 10% advance payment to initiate the design process.

Design Phase

Design Stage

01

Site Measurement

Accurate measurements taken at the project site.

02

Requirement Analysis

Detailed discussion to understand functional and design requirements.

03

Concept Development

Creation of design concepts based on client preferences and lifestyle.

04

Space Planning

Detailed layout planning for optimum space utilization.

05

3D Design Presentation

Presentation of realistic 3D views for better visualization.

06

Detailed Quotation

Preparation of a comprehensive quotation based on finalized designs and materials.

07

Design Finalization

Freeze designs, materials, finishes, and scope of work.

08

Working Drawings

Preparation of detailed 2D drawings for execution.

09

Payment Milestone

40% payment upon approval of designs and drawings.

Execution Phase

Execution Stage

01

Site Preparation

Design marking and layout setting at the site. Floor protection to prevent damage during execution.

02

Civil & Plumbing Works

Civil modifications and plumbing work, if required as per design.

03

False Ceiling Framework

Installation of ceiling channels and framework.

04

Electrical Works

Electrical wiring and point provisions as per approved designs and drawings.

05

False Ceiling Installation

Fixing of Gypsum Boards / POP Boards as per the finalized design.

06

Woodwork Execution

Manufacturing and installation of all carpentry and modular furniture work.

07

Primer / First Coat Painting

Application of the first coat of paint for surface preparation.

08

Payment Milestone

45% payment upon completion of the above execution stage.

Handover Phase

Handover Stage

01

Finishing Works

Completion of all woodwork finishes. Final painting and touch-up works.

02

Electrical & Appliance Installation

Installation of lights, fans, and air conditioners.

03

Surface & Décor Installation

Glass and mirror installation. Corian countertop installation (if applicable). Wallpaper installation. Curtain and blind installation.

04

Furniture Installation

Procurement and installation of loose furniture as per design.

05

Cleaning & Quality Check

Deep cleaning of the entire space. Final cleaning before handover. Snag check and rectification of any pending items.

06

Final Payment

Collection of the remaining 5% payment.

07

Happy Handover

Project completion and handover of your beautifully designed space.

Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

An Interior Designer is a trained professional who creates and designs interior spaces which are aesthetically attractive and functional.

An Interior Decorator, on the other hand, views interior design with a largely cosmetic approach using decorative elements to merely rearrange existing spaces.

Consider this: Whether you're planning new offices, or renovating your old ones, the investment is considerable. Wrong decisions could leave you with expensive mistakes long after the project is completed.

We add value: We'll shed light on new ideas, better ways of doing things and ways to save money. Your spaces will be ready on time and on budget, will look great and will function well.

Good design: We provide a wide variety of choices throughout the design process that assure your new space will reflect your unique personality — not a standard template.

Team approach: We believe in a cooperative effort between the client, designer and other professionals — sharing ideas and creating spaces that balance aesthetics, functionality, ergonomics and economics.

With years of specialized education and training, coupled with practical experience, interior designers are capable of bringing construction costs down considerably. Design solutions that ensure minimum wastage, assisting clients to get discounts from suppliers, and verifying contractors' bills, contribute largely to achieving a reduction in wastage.

As soon as possible. The ideal time is during the planning stage — before actual construction begins. Involving a designer early allows door and window openings, layouts, and structural elements to be planned correctly from the start, saving the cost of structural changes later.

It is in the client's interest to allow the designer to finalise most of the paperwork, identify materials, and confirm contractors before starting actual site work. Rushing the initial stages often leads to increased costs due to delays and ever-changing layouts.

Completion depends on various factors: the scope of work, choice of design, your ability to make decisions from the choices provided, level of planning done before construction, time at your disposal, and flow of funds.

Material procurement is generally left to our clients. However, if desired, we can assist in procuring quality material at the best possible price. We operate strictly on a fee-to-service basis and pass on all professional discounts and commissions to our clients, protecting their interests at all times.

At a retention fee (adjusted in the final amount), we take up site measurements, come up with options for the basic plan, and hold initial meetings to understand each other better. This helps us arrive at the final estimated cost based on your brief and requirements. Our fee is generally a lump sum or per sq.ft basis rather than a percentage, to avoid any influence on total project cost.

Where there is a limited budget, we work backwards and create the best possible design solution within the given cost. We are always steady to our clients' budgets.

There is no such thing as a "right" or "wrong" product — just the product best suited for your requirement. Identify the requirements of your project first, then explore the wide range of designs and colours, and select based on your taste. We guide you through this process every step of the way.
